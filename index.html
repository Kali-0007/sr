Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is Income Tax?

Income Tax is a direct tax levied by the Government of India on your earnings (salary, business, investments, etc.). It is charged at progressive rates – higher income means higher tax.

2. What is the difference between Financial Year (FY) and Assessment Year (AY)?

FY is the year in which you earn income (e.g., FY 2024-25: April 1, 2024 to March 31, 2025). AY is the next year in which that income is assessed for tax (AY 2025-26).

3. What is the difference between Old Tax Regime and New Tax Regime?

New Regime has lower tax rates but most deductions/exemptions are not allowed. Old Regime has higher rates but allows deductions like 80C, HRA, 80D, etc. New Regime is the default; opt out to choose Old.

4. Which deductions are not available in the New Tax Regime?

80C (PPF, ELSS), 80D (health insurance), HRA, home loan interest (self-occupied), LTA, etc. Only standard deduction (₹75,000 for salaried) and a few limited ones are allowed.

5. Which regime is better – Old or New?

If your deductions exceed ₹3-4 lakh, Old is better. For low deductions, New is preferable. Use a tax calculator to compare.

6. When is ITR filing mandatory?

If total income exceeds basic exemption (New: ₹4 lakh, Old: ₹2.5-5 lakh), or you have foreign assets, or high-value expenses (e.g., ₹2 lakh foreign travel). Also file for refund claims or loans/visas.

7. What is the last date for ITR filing for AY 2025-26?

Non-audit cases: September 15, 2025 (extended). Audit cases: October 31. Belated ITR: December 31, 2025 (with late fee).

8. Which ITR form should I use?

- ITR-1 (Sahaj): Salaried, income up to ₹50 lakh, one house property.
- ITR-2: Capital gains, foreign income.
- ITR-3: Business/profession income.
- ITR-4 (Sugam): Presumptive taxation for small businesses.

9. How to file ITR online?

Log in to incometax.gov.in, download pre-filled ITR, fill details, and e-Verify (Aadhaar OTP or net banking).

10. Why is e-Verification mandatory after filing ITR?

If not done within 30 days, the ITR is considered invalid.

11. When can I file Belated or Revised ITR?

Belated: Up to December 31, 2025 (late fee up to ₹5,000). Revised: To correct mistakes, up to December 31, 2025.

12. Do I need to attach documents with ITR?

No, ITR is annexure-less. But keep proofs (Form-16, investment proofs) for potential notices.

13. How much deduction under Section 80C?

Up to ₹1.5 lakh (PPF, ELSS, LIC, tuition fees, etc.) – only in Old Regime.

14. How much deduction for health insurance (80D)?

Self/family: ₹25,000 (₹50,000 for seniors); Parents: additional ₹25,000/₹50,000 – Old Regime only.

15. How much deduction on home loan?

Interest: ₹2 lakh (self-occupied, 24(b)); Principal: under 80C – Old Regime. Unlimited loss set-off for let-out property.

16. How to claim HRA exemption?

For salaried paying rent (Metro: 50% salary, Non-metro: 40%) – Old Regime only.

17. What is the Standard Deduction?

₹75,000 for salaried/pensioners (available in both regimes).

18. When and how do I get tax refund?

Within 30-45 days of processing, directly to bank account. Check status on e-filing portal.

19. When do I need to pay Advance Tax?

If tax liability > ₹10,000, pay in installments: June 15, September, December, March.

20. What are the tax rules for NRIs?

Only India-sourced income is taxable. Foreign income exempt if non-resident.

21. How is Capital Gains taxed?

Short-term: Slab rates; Long-term (equity): 12.5% (exempt up to ₹1.25 lakh).

22. What to do if I receive an Income Tax notice?

Log in to portal, reply with documents. Do not ignore.

23. What is Form-16 and why is it important?

Issued by employer, shows TDS details. Essential for ITR filing.

24. Should I check AIS and Form 26AS before filing ITR?

Yes – they show TDS and high-value transactions. Correct mismatches.

25. Are emails asking for PIN/password from Income Tax Department fake?

Yes, the department never asks for such details. Beware of fraud.
