1. Basics of Income Tax

1. What is Income Tax?

Income Tax is a direct tax levied by the Government of India on the income of individuals, companies, and other entities. It is charged on earnings from salary, business, investments, etc., at progressive rates.

2. What is a Tax Year?

A ‘Tax Year’ is a period of 12 months commencing from 1st April and ending on 31st March of the following year, replacing the term ‘previous year’ in the new Income Tax Act 2025.

3. What is the difference between Gross Total Income and Total Income?

Gross Total Income is the total income from all heads before deductions. Total Income is after deducting eligible exemptions and deductions under Chapter VI-A.

4. Who is a Resident for Tax Purposes?

A person is resident if they stay in India for 182 days or more in the tax year, or 60 days in the year and 365 days in the preceding 4 years, with exceptions for certain cases.

5. What is PAN?

Permanent Account Number (PAN) is a 10-digit alphanumeric identifier issued by the Income Tax Department for tracking financial transactions.

6. What is TAN?

Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number (TAN) is required for entities deducting tax at source (TDS).

7. What is the Role of Form 26AS?

Form 26AS shows TDS, TCS, advance tax, and self-assessment tax paid, now integrated with AIS for comprehensive view.

8. What is AIS?

Annual Information Statement (AIS) provides a detailed view of financial transactions, including those in Form 26AS.

9. What is the New Income Tax Act 2025?

The Income Tax Act 2025 simplifies the old Act, introduces 'Tax Year', and is applicable from April 1, 2025, after Presidential assent.

10. When is Income Tax Payable?

Income tax is payable in advance (advance tax) if liability exceeds Rs. 10,000, or through TDS/TCS, and balance with ITR.

2. Tax Slabs and Regimes (AY 2025-26)

11. What are the New Tax Regime Slabs for FY 2025-26?

Up to Rs. 4L: Nil; Rs. 4-8L: 5%; Rs. 8-12L: 10%; Rs. 12-16L: 15%; Rs. 16-20L: 20%; Rs. 20-24L: 25%; Above 24L: 30%.

12. What is the Old Tax Regime?

Old regime allows deductions like 80C, HRA, but higher rates: Up to Rs. 2.5L Nil (Rs. 3L for seniors), then 5%, 20%, 30%.

13. Is the New Regime Default?

Yes, from AY 2024-25, new regime is default; opt out via Form 10-IEA for old regime.

14. What is Rebate u/s 87A in New Regime?

Rebate up to Rs. 60,000 if income <= Rs. 12L, making it tax-free.

15. What is Marginal Relief?

For income slightly above Rs. 12L, tax is limited to excess over Rs. 12L, e.g., Rs. 12.1L pays only Rs. 10,000 tax.

16. Slabs for Senior Citizens?

In old regime: Up to Rs. 3L Nil (60-80 yrs), Rs. 5L Nil (80+ yrs).

17. Surcharge Rates?

10% for Rs. 50L-1Cr, 15% for 1-2Cr, 25% for 2-5Cr, 37% above 5Cr (capped at 15% for certain incomes).

18. Health & Education Cess?

4% on income tax plus surcharge.

19. Which Regime is Better?

New for low deductions; old if high investments/deductions like 80C > Rs. 1.5L.

20. Standard Deduction in New Regime?

Rs. 75,000 for salaried individuals.

3. ITR Filing

21. Who Must File ITR?

If income > Rs. 2.5L (old) or Rs. 4L (new), or have foreign assets, etc.

22. Due Date for ITR AY 2025-26?

Non-audit: July 31, 2025; Audit: Oct 31; Belated: Dec 31 with fee.

23. Which ITR Form for Salaried?

ITR-1 (Sahaj) if income < Rs. 50L, no capital gains.

24. Documents Needed for ITR?

Form-16, PAN, Aadhaar, bank statements, investment proofs.

25. How to Opt for Old Regime?

File Form 10-IEA before due date u/s 139(1).

26. What is Belated ITR?

ITR filed after due date, allowed till Dec 31 with late fee u/s 234F (Rs. 5,000).

27. Can ITR be Revised?

Yes, within due date u/s 139(5), or updated return u/s 139(8A) till 4 years.

28. What is Pre-filled ITR?

ITR with auto-populated data from AIS/26AS for easy filing.

29. Late Fee for ITR?

Rs. 1,000 if income < Rs. 5L, Rs. 5,000 otherwise, u/s 234F.

30. How to Track ITR Status?

Via e-filing portal using PAN and ACK number.

4. Deductions and Exemptions

31. What is Section 80C Deduction?

Up to Rs. 1.5L for investments in PPF, ELSS, tuition fees (old regime only).

32. HRA Exemption?

Least of actual HRA, rent paid - 10% salary, 50% salary (metro)/40% (non-metro).

33. Section 80D Health Insurance?

Rs. 25,000 for self/family, Rs. 50,000 for parents (old regime).

34. NPS Deduction u/s 80CCD(1B)?

Additional Rs. 50,000 over 80C limit.

35. Education Loan u/s 80E?

Interest paid, no limit, for 8 years.

36. House Loan Principal u/s 80C?

Up to Rs. 1.5L included in 80C.

37. Home Loan Interest u/s 24?

Rs. 2L for self-occupied property.

38. Are Deductions Available in New Regime?

Limited: Standard deduction Rs. 75K, NPS employer contribution, etc.; no 80C, HRA.

39. What is Section 80G?

50% or 100% deduction on donations to specified funds (old regime).

40. Transport Allowance Exemption?

Rs. 1,600 p.m. for differently-abled; otherwise included in salary.

5. Penalties, Appeals & Compliance

41. Penalty for Late ITR Filing?

Rs. 5,000 u/s 234F; Rs. 1,000 if income < Rs. 5L.

42. Interest on Late Tax Payment u/s 234A/B/C?

1% p.m. on outstanding tax from due dates.

43. What is Advance Tax?

Tax paid in installments if liability > Rs. 10,000; due 15th June, Sept, Dec, March.

44. Penalty for Non-Payment of Advance Tax?

Interest u/s 234B/C at 1% p.m. on unpaid amount.

45. How to Appeal Against Assessment?

File appeal to CIT(A) within 30 days, then ITAT, High Court, SC.

46. What is Rectification u/s 154?

Correct errors in assessment order; apply within 4 years.

47. TDS on Salary?

Employer deducts based on projected income, considering deductions.

48. Penalty for Concealment u/s 271(1)(c)?

100-300% of tax evaded.

49. What is Tax Audit u/s 44AB?

Mandatory if business turnover > Rs. 1Cr (10Cr if cash <5%), profession > Rs. 50L.

50. How Secure is ITR Data?

e-Filing uses SSL encryption; comply with data protection laws.
